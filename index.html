Game Overview

Game Name: Legend (Working Title)

Genre: Social Survival Sim / Narrative RPG / Comedy

Core Concept

On your first day at a new school, you discover everyone is against you: the teachers, the parents, the bullies, and even strangers. Your goal is to survive and thrive by building a network of loyal friends, which requires navigating a treacherous social landscape, managing your reputation, and throwing the best parties, all while staying under the radar.

World & Factions

The game world is designed to be a microcosm of suburban teen life, with distinct zones offering different challenges and opportunities. The social ecosystem is the heart of the game, composed of various cliques and authority figures who react to the player's actions.

Key Locations

The Suburbs: Your home base for stealth and chores.
The Heights: Rich area with high-paying jobs.
Northwood High: The central social hub.
The "Badlands": High-risk area for acquiring items.
The Woods/Beach: Off-grid hangout and party spots.
The Skate Park: A specific clique's social hub.

Gameplay Loops

The game is built around two interconnected loops: the daily school schedule and the overarching social progression. These systems create a compelling rhythm of risk, reward, and relationship management.

The School Day: A Typical Loop

1. Pre-Bell: Arrive at school, observe cliques, and initiate conversations.
2. Period Menu: Choose to "Go to Class" (maintain grade) or "Skip Class" (free-roam, lower grade).
3. Between Periods: Short window to navigate hallways, talk to NPCs, and trigger events.
4. After School: Free-roam period for jobs, hangouts, and exploration.
5. Curfew: Must be home by 8 PM to avoid raising parent suspicion and getting grounded.

The Core Social Loop

1. Make Friends: Succeed in dialogue minigames to get phone numbers.
2. Go Out: Get invited to or initiate hangouts at various locations.
3. Maintain Friendships: Avoid negative events like standing up friends or siding against them.
4. Acquire Resources: Earn money and buy social lubricants to fund your social life.
5. Throw Parties: The ultimate social goal, leveraging your network and resources.

Core Systems

These are the underlying mechanics that drive the gameplay loops, providing depth and player choice.

Economy & Money-Making

Players have multiple paths to earn money, each with its own risks and rewards. Choose your hustle to fund your social ambitions.

The Straight & Narrow
The Side Hustle
The Bully

Jobs App: Use a "Facepage" app on your phone to find legitimate jobs like mowing lawns. Each job is a quick, fun minigame.

Tutoring: If your grades are high enough, you can tutor other students for a significant cash reward.

Item Flipping: Observe NPCs to see what items they desire. Find or buy these items from a "Flipping App" and sell them for a profit.

Intimidation: A high-risk, high-reward path. Intimidate other students to get cash and completed homework, but risk damaging your reputation and attracting unwanted attention.

Stealth & Evasion

Staying under the radar of parents and teachers is crucial for survival. These mechanics focus on concealment and acting sober when you're not.

Hiding Your Stash

Your parents can randomly search your room. You must find and use hiding spots (e.g., under the bed, in a loose floorboard) for your drugs and other illicit items. The better the hiding spot, the lower the chance of discovery. Getting caught results in being grounded and having items confiscated.

Sobriety Check Minigames

If you come home after using consumables, your parents might get suspicious and trigger a minigame. Failure increases their suspicion meter, eventually leading to being grounded.

Breathing Game

A rhythm-based minigame where you must click in time with an on-screen prompt to simulate steady breathing.

Stand Still Game

Use your mouse or controller to keep your character balanced within a small circle, fighting against swaying effects.

Delete Emails Game

A time-pressured challenge to quickly find and delete incriminating emails or messages from your phone/computer before your parents see them.

Drugs & Consumables

Consumables act as "social lubricants" or provide temporary stat boosts. They can be bought, found, or received as gifts. Using them carries risks, such as addiction, negative reputation, or getting caught by authority figures.

Alcohol

Effect: Lowers dialogue difficulty at parties.
Risk: "Sloppy" reputation debuff if overused. Triggers Sobriety Checks.

Weed

Effect: Unlocks unique dialogue options with Druggies/Roadmen.
Risk: Can cause "lazy" debuff, slowing movement. Triggers Sobriety Checks.

"Study Pills"

Effect: Temporary boost to grades/tutoring income.
Risk: High chance of addiction, leading to stat penalties if not consumed.

Heist System

Heists are large-scale missions requiring a team. Recruiting members involves building trust within their respective cliques.

The Path to Recruitment

1. Complete Clique Quests: Do favors for a target clique (e.g., help a Nerd with homework, win a sports minigame for a Jock).
2. Build Trust Meter: Each completed quest increases your "Trust" with that clique and key members.
3. Recruit Specialist: Once trust is high enough, you can approach a specific member and ask them to join your heist crew.

UI/UX Design

The UI is designed to be clean, intuitive, and mobile-inspired, centering all key interactions around the player's phone.

The Phone

The central menu hub. Access apps for Jobs ("Facepage"), Social contacts, Item-flipping, and viewing your Reputation status.

Dialogue Interface

A choice-based system with timed responses. Options are colored based on the associated social skill (e.g., Intimidation, Charm).

In-Game HUD

Minimalist display showing current time, money, and a small icon indicating your current reputation level (e.g., thumbs up/down).

Endgame

The ultimate objective that drives the player's journey from start to finish.

The Ultimate Goal

Go from a lonely newcomer to a social legend with a strong circle of loyal friends. Use your network, reputation, and resources to throw the most legendary end-of-year party the school has ever seen.
